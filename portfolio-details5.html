5 Easy Money Habit Changes to Save More Money

The hardest part about saving money is getting started. Perhaps that’s why 60 percent of American millennials don’t even have enough in their savings to cover a $1,000 emergency. Crazy, isn’t it?

Saving money doesn’t have to be that hard. It’s the little things that can actually make all the difference. Here are our top five easy habit changes that can help you save more money.

1. Brew your own coffee (Potential annual savings: Up to $960!)

The average American spends $1,100 every year (or $3 per day) on coffee, according to a 2017 “Money that Matters” report. If you live in a bigger city, it could easily add up $6 a day for your quick on-the-go latte. This would be $2,200 a year!

Invest in a good coffee machine and brew your own coffee at home - you’ll soon see your savings pile up. As an additional bonus - you’re more environmentally friendly brewing your own coffee as well!

2. Cancel unused subscriptions (Potential annual savings: Up to $480)

A report found that nearly one in 10 young people spend an average of $200 per month on subscription services like Spotify, Hulu, Netflix, iTunes and Apple Music. From the free trial you forgot to cancel to the Birchbox membership you’re still paying for - this can add up really fast.

Cut back just 20% off of that monthly $200 and you could save $480 a year. We suggest using Truebill to identify any unwanted subscriptions.

3. Set up a routine automatic savings transfer, even if it’s only $20 a week (Potential annual savings $960)

The only way for automated savings to work is if you set it and forget about it. It really is that simple. You set the system up once and then let the money slowly accumulate.You probably won’t even notice the $20 every week but in 5 years it will add up to $4800 - pretty cool to save almost five thousand dollars without even thinking about it.

4. Cook your own meals instead of eating out (Potential annual savings $1000)

Spending just $50 on eating out per week adds up to $2500 a year? Let’s be honest - it’s often easy to spend way more than that.

If you go out for lunch Monday through Friday for a year, you might spend $10 a meal – a pretty good deal at most dining establishments. This already adds up to $50 weekly. Not even mentioning if you go out to dinner two or three times a week. And yes, if you order takeout or delivery, this still counts as “dining out” with all the delivery surcharges.

No, you don’t have to stop eating out or ordering an occasional UberEats order. Even if you only skip one moderate restaurant meal a week in favor of cooking or leftovers, that’s about $1000 in annual savings.

Keep each other accountable, have some healthy competition and get rewarded by the app (more free money!) if you both finish your goal successfully.
